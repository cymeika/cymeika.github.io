My Study Abroad Adventure in Taiwan

Read time: 3 minutes

Kaohsiung Experience:

During my study abroad in Taiwan, I had the incredible opportunity to immerse myself in the vibrant city of Kaohsiung. Living on campus at my university allowed me to deeply engage with both academic and local life. Kaohsiung, known for its lively night markets and beautiful harbor, became my home away from home. The city's blend of modernity and tradition created a unique backdrop for my coding classes, making each day an enriching experience.

"Taiwan is a beautiful blend of tradition and innovation!" - Cymeika, 2024

Exploring Kaohsiung’s diverse neighborhoods was a highlight of my stay. From the bustling Liuhe Night Market to the serene Lotus Pond, every part of the city offered something new. The local food was a culinary adventure, with street vendors and small eateries serving up delicious Taiwanese specialties. I also enjoyed leisurely walks along the Love River, where the blend of urban energy and natural beauty was truly captivating.

Another memorable aspect of my time in Kaohsiung was engaging with the local community. Participating in university events and local festivals allowed me to experience Taiwanese culture firsthand. Whether it was a traditional performance or a modern art exhibit, these experiences enriched my understanding of Taiwan’s dynamic cultural landscape.

Exploring Taipei:

My journey also took me to Taipei, Taiwan’s bustling capital. From its impressive skyline to its rich cultural landmarks, Taipei offered a dynamic contrast to Kaohsiung’s laid-back vibe. I explored historic temples, modern shopping districts, and enjoyed the city's renowned street food.

"Taipei is a city where the ancient and modern coexist in harmony, creating a vibrant mosaic of experiences." - Cymeika, 2024

The Taipei 101 skyscraper was a standout experience, offering breathtaking views of the city from its observation deck. Wandering through the night markets, such as Shilin and Raohe, was an adventure in itself, with an endless array of snacks and goods to discover. Taipei’s blend of modernity and tradition created a fascinating urban landscape that was both vibrant and historical.

Additionally, my time in Taipei included visits to some of the city's beautiful parks and cultural institutions. The National Palace Museum provided a deep dive into Taiwan’s rich history and art, while the serene Chiang Kai-shek Memorial Hall offered a peaceful escape from the city’s hustle and bustle. These visits added a rich layer to my understanding of Taiwan’s heritage.

Discovering Tainan

In Tainan, I had the rewarding experience of teaching English to local students. This cultural exchange was both fulfilling and eye-opening, as it allowed me to connect with the community in a meaningful way. The enthusiasm and curiosity of the students made each lesson a joy, and I cherished the opportunity to contribute to their learning.

"In Tainan, every corner tells a story of the past, making it a captivating destination for history enthusiasts and cultural explorers." - Cymeika, 2024

Tainan is also known for its rich history and traditional architecture. Visiting ancient temples and historic sites provided a fascinating glimpse into Taiwan’s past. The city’s charming streets and traditional cuisine made my time there truly memorable. Trying local delicacies and participating in cultural activities offered a deep appreciation for Tainan’s unique heritage.

In addition to teaching and exploring historical sites, I had the chance to practice Chinese calligraphy in Tainan. This art form allowed me to connect with Taiwan’s cultural heritage on a personal level, as I learned the delicate techniques and expressions of this traditional craft. The experience was both meditative and educational, adding a special touch to my travels.
